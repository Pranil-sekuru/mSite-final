About mSITE

Mobile Social Interaction Therapy by Exposure — a pervasive computing system for addressing social isolation in serious mental illness.

The Problem

Serious Mental Illness (SMI) — including schizophrenia, schizoaffective disorder, and bipolar I with psychosis — affects millions worldwide. A defining feature of SMI is social isolation, which worsens negative symptoms and dramatically reduces quality of life.

Existing interventions suffer from poor timing: they're delivered during scheduled clinic visits or at random times, missing the real moments when isolation occurs. There is no way for clinicians to know when a patient is alone and struggling.

 The Innovation

mSITE is the first context-aware intervention targeting social outcomes in SMI. Rather than relying on self-report or scheduled reminders, a passive sensing pipeline detects when a participant is:

Away from home (GPS geofencing) Alone — low conversation count detected by microphone

When both conditions are met, the system fires a personalized EMA (Ecological Momentary Assessment) prompt using CBT principles, with therapist-authored messages embedded.

 Blended Approach

mSITE combines two therapeutic modalities:

Weeks 1–4 In-Person CBT Face-to-face cognitive-behavioral therapy sessions where clinicians personalize challenge messages for defeatist and threat beliefs. Weeks 5–8 Remote + Mobile CBT Remote coaching sessions combined with continuous context-aware mobile interventions triggered by the sensing pipeline.
